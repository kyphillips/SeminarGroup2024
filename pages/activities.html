Activities

Wayne State is offering a number of both academic and sports camps this summer. Wayne State College will host a variety of camps this summer that will provide a tremendous opportunity for students to get a taste of a wide range of professional programs of study. The camps are affordable and offer the chance for students of all ages to immerse themselves in music, filmmaking, criminal justice, engineering, nursing, and athletics.

Summer Music Camp

  • The Wayne State College Summer Music Camp is the perfect opportunity to explore your musical interests in a fun and positive environment.
    You'll get a variety of experiences while learning from professional musicians who are experts in their fields
  • Participation in large ensembles (bands and choirs) as well as smaller ensembles (Mariachi)
  • Classes in Ukulele, World Drumming, Music Theater, Songwriting, and Piano
  • Evening social activities such as dancing, scavenger hunts, kickball, and more
  • Private lessons from WSC faculty and other professional musicians
  • Open to students who have completed grades 5-12.

Cost

Resident campers: $350 (includes meals and housing)

Commuting campers: $300 (includes meals but no housing)

FilmMakers Workshop

A great opportunity to get firsthand experience in filmmaking! Provides an introduction in filmmaking led by WSC professor Dr. Michael White. Designed for teens. Plan, shoot, and edit your own short films. All facets of production will be covered, including: casting, set design, location scouting, lighting, shooting, and editing

Open to students in grades 7-12

Brought to you by Wayne State College and the University of Nebraska-Lincoln College of Engineering.
